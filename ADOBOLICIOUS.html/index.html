How to Cook Adobo

Chicken Adobo is an authentic Filipino dish and is one of the mostly recognized Filipino foods. Not to be mistaken with Mexican adobo, this dish is uniquely prepared by stewing chicken in vinegar and soy sauce.

According to several experts in Asian food history, Filipinos were cooking adobo before Spanish colonization. Cooking with vinegar preserves meat, and is one of the earliest food preservation practices. This is also true of pork adobo, which is another popular variation of the dish.

To cook adobo(pork or chicken), you will need:

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg chicken or pork (or a mix of both), cut into pieces
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup vinegar
  • 1 cup water
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon black peppercorns
  • 3 bay leaves
  • 1 tablespoon cooking oil
  • 1 teaspoon sugar (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a pot, heat oil and saut√© garlic until fragrant.
  2. Add the meat and cook until lightly browned.
  3. Pour in the soy sauce, vinegar, and water.
  4. Add bay leaves and peppercorns.
  5. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and let it simmer for 30-40 minutes.
  6. If desired, add sugar for a hint of sweetness.
  7. Continue simmering until the sauce thickens.
  8. Serve hot with rice and enjoy!
